Fallacist’s Fallacy

A logical fallacy

The fallacist’s fallacy occurs when someone dismisses an argument entirely because it contains a logical fallacy. While the presence of a fallacy weakens an argument, it does not necessarily mean that the conclusion is false.

For example, "Since your argument contains a straw man fallacy, your conclusion must be wrong" is a fallacist’s fallacy because it assumes the conclusion is false based solely on the presence of a fallacy.

Critical thinking involves evaluating the entire argument and its evidence, rather than dismissing it outright due to a logical fallacy. By doing so, we can avoid the fallacist’s fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Straw Man

Examples

Business Example: "Your proposal includes a slippery slope fallacy, so it can't be taken seriously."

Academic Example: "This theory has a false analogy fallacy, so the entire theory is invalid."

Pop-Culture Example: "In debates, characters often dismiss each other’s arguments entirely due to one fallacy."

Intimate Partner Example: "Your argument contains a false dilemma, so I won't consider your suggestion."
