Diagnosis Fallacy

A logical fallacy

The diagnosis fallacy occurs when someone mistakes the identification of a problem for its solution. This fallacy shifts the focus from understanding a problem to assuming that identifying it is equivalent to solving it.

For example, "We've identified that the team is not communicating effectively. Therefore, our work is done" is an example of the diagnosis fallacy because it assumes that identifying the issue resolves it.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than relying on identification alone. By doing so, we can avoid the diagnosis fallacy and maintain a focus on both understanding and addressing issues.
