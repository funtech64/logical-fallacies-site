Complex Question Fallacy

A logical fallacy

A complex question fallacy occurs when someone asks a question that contains an assumption, making it difficult to answer without accepting the assumption. This fallacy shifts the focus from clear reasoning to a complex question.

For example, "Have you stopped cheating on your exams?" is a complex question fallacy because it assumes that the person has been cheating.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and clear reasoning, rather than complex questions. By doing so, we can avoid the complex question fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Loaded Question

Examples

Business Example: "Have you stopped using unethical practices in your business?"

Academic Example: "Have you stopped plagiarizing your papers?"

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use complex questions to create tension or confusion."

Intimate Partner Example: "Have you stopped lying to me?"
