Moralistic Fallacy

A logical fallacy

The moralistic fallacy occurs when someone assumes that what ought to be, morally speaking, is what is actually the case. This fallacy shifts the focus from sound reasoning to assumptions based on moral beliefs.

For example, "Because it would be nice if everyone were kind, everyone must be kind" is an example of the moralistic fallacy because it assumes that reality conforms to moral ideals.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than assumptions based on moral beliefs. By doing so, we can avoid the moralistic fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Naturalistic Fallacy

Examples

Business Example: "Our product should be successful because it's ethically made."

Academic Example: "Students should be motivated by the love of learning, so they must be."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use the moralistic fallacy to justify their beliefs."

Intimate Partner Example: "You should trust me because I am your partner, so you must trust me."
