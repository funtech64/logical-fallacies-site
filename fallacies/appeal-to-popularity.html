Appeal to Popularity

A logical fallacy

An appeal to popularity occurs when someone argues that a belief or action is correct because it is popular. This fallacy shifts the focus from evidence and logic to popular opinion.

For example, "Everyone is using this product, so it must be the best" is an appeal to popularity because it relies on popularity rather than evidence of quality.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than popular opinion. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to popularity fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Bandwagon Fallacy

Examples

Business Example: "Our product is the best-selling, so it must be the best."

Academic Example: "This theory is widely accepted, so it must be true."

Pop-Culture Example: "In advertisements, products are often presented as popular to persuade people."

Intimate Partner Example: "Everyone else is doing it, so we should too."
