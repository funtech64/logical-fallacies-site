Loaded Question

A logical fallacy

A loaded question is a question that contains a controversial or unjustified assumption. It shifts the focus from answering the question to addressing the assumption.

For example, "Have you stopped cheating on your exams?" assumes that the person has been cheating, regardless of their answer.

Critical thinking involves recognizing and addressing the assumptions in loaded questions. By doing so, we can avoid the loaded question fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Begging the Question

Examples

Business Example: "Why are you so bad at managing projects?"

Academic Example: "Why did you fail to include proper citations in your paper?"

Pop-Culture Example: "In movies, characters often ask loaded questions to provoke a reaction."

Intimate Partner Example: "Why don't you care about our relationship anymore?"
