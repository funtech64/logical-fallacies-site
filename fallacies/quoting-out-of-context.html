Quoting Out of Context

A logical fallacy

Quoting out of context occurs when someone presents a quote without its surrounding context, which changes its intended meaning. This fallacy shifts the focus from the original message to a misrepresented version.

For example, "The president said, 'We cannot afford to care for every American,' omitting the rest of the quote that explains the context, is quoting out of context.

Critical thinking involves considering the full context of a quote to understand its true meaning. By doing so, we can avoid the quoting out of context fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Straw Man

Examples

Business Example: "Our competitor claims, 'Our product has flaws,' but omits that they were discussing early prototypes."

Academic Example: "A study claims, 'The results are inconclusive,' but omits the explanation of the limitations."

Pop-Culture Example: "In news articles, quotes are often taken out of context to create sensational headlines."

Intimate Partner Example: "You said, 'I don't love you,' omitting that you were quoting a movie we watched."
