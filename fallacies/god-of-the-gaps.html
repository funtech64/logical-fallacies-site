God of the Gaps

A logical fallacy

The god of the gaps fallacy occurs when gaps in scientific knowledge are taken as evidence for the existence of God or the supernatural. This fallacy shifts the focus from scientific inquiry to supernatural explanations.

For example, "Science can't explain how life began, so it must have been created by God" is a god of the gaps fallacy because it uses a gap in knowledge as evidence for a supernatural explanation.

Critical thinking involves seeking natural explanations and acknowledging the limits of current knowledge without resorting to supernatural explanations. By doing so, we can avoid the god of the gaps fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Argument from Ignorance

Examples

Business Example: "We can't explain the success of our competitor, so it must be luck."

Academic Example: "The origins of this phenomenon are unknown, so it must be supernatural."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often attribute unexplained events to ghosts or other supernatural forces."

Intimate Partner Example: "We can't explain why our relationship is struggling, so it must be fate."
