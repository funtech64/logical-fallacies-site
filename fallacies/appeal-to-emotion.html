Appeal to Emotion

A logical fallacy

An appeal to emotion occurs when someone manipulates people's emotions to win an argument, rather than using evidence and logical reasoning. This fallacy shifts the focus from the argument's merits to emotional manipulation.

For example, "If you don't donate to our cause, thousands of animals will die" is an appeal to emotion because it relies on emotional impact rather than evidence of effectiveness.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than emotional appeals. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to emotion fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Pity

Examples

Business Example: "Our product is the best choice because it will make you feel happier."

Academic Example: "This theory must be true because it offers hope for the future."

Pop-Culture Example: "In movies, characters often use emotional appeals to persuade others."

Intimate Partner Example: "You should stay with me because I can't live without you."
