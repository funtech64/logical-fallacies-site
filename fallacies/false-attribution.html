False Attribution

A logical fallacy

False attribution occurs when someone incorrectly attributes a statement, idea, or finding to a source that did not produce it. This fallacy shifts the focus from accurate information to misinformation.

For example, "Einstein said that creativity is more important than knowledge" is an example of false attribution if Einstein never made such a statement.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on accurate information and credible sources, rather than misinformation. By doing so, we can avoid the false attribution fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: False Consensus Effect

Examples

Business Example: "Our product is endorsed by a famous celebrity" (when it is not).

Academic Example: "This theory is supported by leading scientists" (when it is not).

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use false attribution to persuade others."

Intimate Partner Example: "You should trust me because I read it in a book" (when the book says something different).
