Cultural Relativism

A logical fallacy

Cultural relativism occurs when someone argues that the beliefs and practices of a culture cannot be judged by the standards of another culture. This fallacy shifts the focus from universal reasoning to cultural differences.

For example, "You can't criticize our traditions because they're part of our culture" is an example of cultural relativism because it exempts cultural practices from scrutiny.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on universal reasoning and evidence, rather than cultural differences. By doing so, we can avoid the cultural relativism fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Subjectivist Fallacy

Examples

Business Example: "Our business practices can't be criticized because they are part of our corporate culture."

Academic Example: "This theory can't be questioned because it is based on cultural beliefs."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use cultural relativism to avoid criticism."

Intimate Partner Example: "You can't criticize my behavior because it's part of who I am."
