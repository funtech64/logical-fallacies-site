Appeal to Spite

A logical fallacy

An appeal to spite occurs when someone attempts to win an argument by exploiting existing feelings of bitterness or resentment. This fallacy shifts the focus from the argument's merits to negative emotions.

For example, "Don't vote for this candidate because they supported a policy that hurt you in the past" is an appeal to spite because it relies on resentment rather than the candidate's current qualifications.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than spite. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to spite fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Emotion

Examples

Business Example: "Don't buy from our competitor because they treated you badly before."

Academic Example: "Don't trust this research because the author once criticized your work."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use spite to manipulate others."

Intimate Partner Example: "Don't listen to your friend because they always take your partner's side."
