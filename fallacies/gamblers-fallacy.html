Gambler’s Fallacy

A logical fallacy

The gambler’s fallacy occurs when someone believes that past random events can influence future random events. This fallacy shifts the focus from statistical probability to false patterns.

For example, "I've lost the last five coin tosses, so I'm due for a win on the next one" is a gambler’s fallacy because it assumes past losses influence future outcomes.

Critical thinking involves understanding and applying the principles of probability and statistics, rather than relying on false patterns. By doing so, we can avoid the gambler’s fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Hot Hand Fallacy

Examples

Business Example: "Our sales have been down for months, so we're due for a big surge soon."

Academic Example: "I've failed the last three tests, so I'm bound to pass the next one."

Pop-Culture Example: "In gambling movies, characters often believe they are due for a win after a losing streak."

Intimate Partner Example: "We've had a string of bad dates, so our next one has to be good."
