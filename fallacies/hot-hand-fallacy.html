Hot Hand Fallacy

A logical fallacy

The hot hand fallacy occurs when someone believes that a person who has experienced success with a random event has a higher chance of continued success. This fallacy shifts the focus from statistical probability to false patterns.

For example, "I've been winning at poker all night, so I'm sure to keep winning" is a hot hand fallacy because it assumes past success influences future outcomes.

Critical thinking involves understanding and applying the principles of probability and statistics, rather than relying on false patterns. By doing so, we can avoid the hot hand fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

Examples

Business Example: "Our sales have been great for the past few months, so they will continue to rise."

Academic Example: "I've aced the last few exams, so I'll definitely ace the next one."

Pop-Culture Example: "In gambling movies, characters often believe they are on a winning streak."

Intimate Partner Example: "We've had a series of great dates, so our relationship will keep improving."
