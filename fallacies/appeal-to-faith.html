Appeal to Faith

A logical fallacy

An appeal to faith occurs when someone argues that a belief or action is correct because it is based on faith, rather than evidence and logical reasoning. This fallacy shifts the focus from evidence and logic to faith.

For example, "You should believe in this because it is a matter of faith" is an appeal to faith because it relies on faith rather than evidence.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than faith. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to faith fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Authority

Examples

Business Example: "This strategy must be good because it is based on faith in our company's vision."

Academic Example: "This theory must be true because it aligns with my faith."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use faith to justify their actions."

Intimate Partner Example: "You should trust me because it is a matter of faith."
