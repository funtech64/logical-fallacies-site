Appeal to Pity

A logical fallacy

An appeal to pity occurs when someone tries to win support for an argument by exploiting the audience's feelings of pity or guilt. This fallacy shifts the focus from the argument's merits to evoking sympathy.

For example, "You must give me a passing grade because I've had a really tough year" is an appeal to pity because it relies on sympathy rather than academic performance.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than pity. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to pity fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Emotion

Examples

Business Example: "You should buy our product because our company is struggling financially."

Academic Example: "Please extend the deadline because I've been dealing with personal issues."

Pop-Culture Example: "In movies, characters often use pity to manipulate others."

Intimate Partner Example: "You should stay with me because I can't live without you."
