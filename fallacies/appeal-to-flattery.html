Appeal to Flattery

A logical fallacy

An appeal to flattery occurs when someone uses compliments or flattery to win support for an argument, rather than using evidence and logical reasoning. This fallacy shifts the focus from the argument's merits to flattery.

For example, "You are such a smart person; you must agree with me" is an appeal to flattery because it relies on compliments rather than evidence.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than flattery. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to flattery fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Emotion

Examples

Business Example: "You're such a valued customer, I know you'll want to take advantage of this offer."

Academic Example: "You're one of the brightest students, so you should support this theory."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use flattery to get what they want."

Intimate Partner Example: "You're so understanding, I know you'll forgive me."
