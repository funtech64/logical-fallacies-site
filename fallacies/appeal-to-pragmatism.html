Appeal to Pragmatism

A logical fallacy

An appeal to pragmatism occurs when someone argues that a belief or action is correct because it is practical or effective, rather than being true or right. This fallacy shifts the focus from truth to practicality.

For example, "We should adopt this policy because it will work, even if it's not entirely fair" is an appeal to pragmatism because it relies on practicality rather than fairness or truth.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than practicality alone. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to pragmatism fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Consequences

Examples

Business Example: "This strategy will increase profits, so we should use it, regardless of its ethical implications."

Academic Example: "This approach will yield results, so we should adopt it, even if it's not entirely accurate."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use pragmatism to justify questionable actions."

Intimate Partner Example: "We should do this because it will solve our problem, even if it's not fair."
