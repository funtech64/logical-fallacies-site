Appeal to Fear

A logical fallacy

An appeal to fear occurs when someone uses fear to persuade people to accept an argument, rather than using evidence and logical reasoning. This fallacy shifts the focus from the argument's merits to fear and anxiety.

For example, "If we don't pass this law, our society will fall into chaos" is an appeal to fear because it relies on fear rather than evidence of the law's necessity.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than fear. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to fear fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Emotion

Examples

Business Example: "You need our security system because burglars could break in at any moment."

Academic Example: "If we don't adopt this new teaching method, our students will fall behind."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use fear to manipulate others."

Intimate Partner Example: "If you leave me, I'll be all alone and miserable."
