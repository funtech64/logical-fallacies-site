Special Pleading

A logical fallacy

Special pleading occurs when someone applies rules or standards to others while exempting themselves without providing adequate justification. This fallacy shifts the focus from fair reasoning to unjustified exceptions.

For example, "I know that most people should follow this rule, but I have a special reason why it doesn't apply to me" is an example of special pleading because it exempts the speaker without justification.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on consistent and fair standards, rather than unjustified exceptions. By doing so, we can avoid the special pleading fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Double-Think

Examples

Business Example: "Our company should be exempt from this regulation because we have unique circumstances."

Academic Example: "My paper should be graded differently because I had a lot of other work."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use special pleading to justify their actions."

Intimate Partner Example: "I shouldn't have to do the chores because I'm tired."
