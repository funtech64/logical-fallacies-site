Red Herring

A logical fallacy

The red herring fallacy occurs when someone introduces an irrelevant topic into an argument to distract from the original issue. This fallacy shifts the focus from the main argument to a side issue.

For example, "We shouldn't worry about climate change because there's poverty to deal with" is a red herring because it diverts attention from climate change to poverty.

Critical thinking involves staying focused on the main argument and not being distracted by irrelevant issues. By doing so, we can avoid the red herring fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Straw Man

Examples

Business Example: "We shouldn't focus on this product defect because our sales are up."

Academic Example: "We don't need to discuss the errors in the study because the findings are interesting."

Pop-Culture Example: "In detective stories, characters often use red herrings to mislead others."

Intimate Partner Example: "We shouldn't discuss our argument because we need to plan our vacation."
