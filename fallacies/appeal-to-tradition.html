Appeal to Tradition

A logical fallacy

An appeal to tradition occurs when someone argues that a belief or action is correct because it is traditional or has been done for a long time. This fallacy shifts the focus from evidence and logic to tradition.

For example, "We have always done it this way, so it must be the best way" is an appeal to tradition because it relies on tradition rather than evidence of effectiveness.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than tradition. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to tradition fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Novelty

Examples

Business Example: "Our company has always used this method, so it must be the best."

Academic Example: "This theory must be true because it has been accepted for centuries."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use tradition to justify their actions."

Intimate Partner Example: "We should continue this practice because it's a family tradition."
