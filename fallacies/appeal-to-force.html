Appeal to Force

A logical fallacy

An appeal to force occurs when someone uses threats or intimidation to win an argument, rather than using evidence and logical reasoning. This fallacy shifts the focus from the argument's merits to force and coercion.

For example, "You should agree with me, or I'll hurt you" is an appeal to force because it relies on threats rather than evidence.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than force. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to force fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Fear

Examples

Business Example: "You should follow my plan, or I'll fire you."

Academic Example: "You should accept this theory, or I'll fail you."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use threats to get their way."

Intimate Partner Example: "You should do what I want, or I'll leave you."
