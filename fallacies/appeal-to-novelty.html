Appeal to Novelty

A logical fallacy

An appeal to novelty occurs when someone argues that a belief or action is correct because it is new or modern. This fallacy shifts the focus from evidence and logic to novelty.

For example, "This new diet plan must be the best because it is the latest trend" is an appeal to novelty because it relies on novelty rather than evidence of effectiveness.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than novelty. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to novelty fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Tradition

Examples

Business Example: "Our new product is the best because it has the latest technology."

Academic Example: "This new theory must be true because it is the most recent."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use novelty to persuade others."

Intimate Partner Example: "We should try this new activity because it's the latest trend."
