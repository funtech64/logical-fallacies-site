Appeal to Common Practice

A logical fallacy

An appeal to common practice occurs when someone argues that a belief or action is correct because it is commonly practiced. This fallacy shifts the focus from evidence and logic to common practice.

For example, "Everyone cheats on their taxes, so it must be okay" is an appeal to common practice because it relies on common behavior rather than ethical reasoning.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than common practice. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to common practice fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Popularity

Examples

Business Example: "All companies in our industry use this accounting method, so it must be acceptable."

Academic Example: "All students plagiarize to some extent, so it must be okay."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often justify their actions by appealing to common practice."

Intimate Partner Example: "Everyone else is doing it, so we should too."
