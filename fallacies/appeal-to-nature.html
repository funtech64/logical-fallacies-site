Appeal to Nature

A logical fallacy

An appeal to nature occurs when someone argues that a belief or action is correct because it is natural. This fallacy shifts the focus from evidence and logic to naturalness.

For example, "This medicine must be good because it is made from natural ingredients" is an appeal to nature because it relies on naturalness rather than evidence of effectiveness.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than naturalness. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to nature fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Naturalistic Fallacy

Examples

Business Example: "Our product is better because it is made from natural materials."

Academic Example: "This theory must be true because it aligns with natural laws."

Pop-Culture Example: "In advertisements, products are often marketed as natural to persuade people."

Intimate Partner Example: "We should take this natural remedy because it's better than synthetic ones."
