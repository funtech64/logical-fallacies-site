Equivocation on God

A logical fallacy

An equivocation on God occurs when someone uses the word "God" in different senses within the same argument, leading to confusion and ambiguity. This fallacy shifts the focus from clear reasoning to equivocation.

For example, "God is love, and love is powerful, so God must be powerful" is an equivocation on God because it relies on different meanings of the word "God."

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and clear reasoning, rather than equivocation. By doing so, we can avoid the equivocation on God fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Equivocation Fallacy

Examples

Business Example: "Our product is divine, and divine products are superior, so our product is superior."

Academic Example: "This term has multiple meanings, so both interpretations must be correct."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use equivocation on God to create humor or confusion."

Intimate Partner Example: "You said you believed in God, but now you're questioning your faith."
