Naturalistic Fallacy

A logical fallacy

The naturalistic fallacy occurs when someone assumes that what is natural is inherently good or right. This fallacy shifts the focus from logical reasoning to the appeal of nature.

For example, "Because this remedy is natural, it must be good for you" is a naturalistic fallacy because it assumes naturalness equates to goodness.

Critical thinking involves evaluating the evidence and reasoning behind claims, rather than relying on the appeal of nature. By doing so, we can avoid the naturalistic fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Moralistic Fallacy

Examples

Business Example: "Our product is better because it's made from all-natural ingredients."

Academic Example: "This theory must be correct because it aligns with natural laws."

Pop-Culture Example: "In movies, characters often believe that natural solutions are always the best."

Intimate Partner Example: "We should follow this natural remedy because it's natural."
