Extra Term Fallacy

A logical fallacy

The extra term fallacy occurs when an argument contains an additional term that invalidates the conclusion. This fallacy shifts the focus from sound reasoning to misleading terms.

For example, "All cats are animals. Some animals are dogs. Therefore, all cats are dogs" is an extra term fallacy because it introduces an additional term that leads to an incorrect conclusion.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on sound reasoning and accurate terms, rather than misleading terms. By doing so, we can avoid the extra term fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Equivocation Fallacy

Examples

Business Example: "All managers are employees. Some employees are interns. Therefore, all managers are interns."

Academic Example: "All mammals are animals. Some animals are reptiles. Therefore, all mammals are reptiles."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use extra term fallacies to create confusion."

Intimate Partner Example: "All friends are people. Some people are strangers. Therefore, all friends are strangers."
