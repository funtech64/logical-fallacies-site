Appeal to Poverty

A logical fallacy

An appeal to poverty occurs when someone argues that a belief or action is correct because it is associated with poverty or humility. This fallacy shifts the focus from evidence and logic to poverty or humility.

For example, "This idea must be true because it comes from a humble person" is an appeal to poverty because it relies on poverty or humility rather than evidence.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than poverty or humility. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to poverty fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Wealth

Examples

Business Example: "This strategy must be good because it was developed by a small, humble company."

Academic Example: "This theory must be true because it was proposed by a modest scientist."

Pop-Culture Example: "In movies, characters often use poverty to gain sympathy and support."

Intimate Partner Example: "You should trust me because I'm not wealthy and have nothing to gain."
