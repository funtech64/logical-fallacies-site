Division Fallacy

A logical fallacy

The division fallacy occurs when someone assumes that what is true for the whole is also true for its parts. This fallacy shifts the focus from valid reasoning to incorrect assumptions about parts and wholes.

For example, "The team is highly skilled, so each member must be highly skilled" is a division fallacy because it assumes that the team's overall skill applies to each individual member.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on logical reasoning and evidence, rather than incorrect assumptions about parts and wholes. By doing so, we can avoid the division fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Composition Fallacy

Examples

Business Example: "Our company is successful, so each department must be successful."

Academic Example: "The book is well-written, so each chapter must be well-written."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use division fallacies to make assumptions."

Intimate Partner Example: "Our relationship is perfect, so each aspect of our relationship must be perfect."
