Appeal to Wealth

A logical fallacy

An appeal to wealth occurs when someone argues that a belief or action is correct because it is associated with wealth or status. This fallacy shifts the focus from evidence and logic to wealth or status.

For example, "This idea must be true because it comes from a wealthy person" is an appeal to wealth because it relies on wealth or status rather than evidence.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than wealth or status. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to wealth fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Poverty

Examples

Business Example: "This strategy must be good because it was developed by a successful, wealthy company."

Academic Example: "This theory must be true because it was proposed by a renowned scientist."

Pop-Culture Example: "In movies, characters often use wealth to gain respect and influence."

Intimate Partner Example: "You should trust me because I'm wealthy and have a lot to lose."
