Appeal to False Authority

A logical fallacy

An appeal to false authority occurs when someone argues that a belief or action is correct because it is endorsed by a figure who is not a legitimate authority on the subject. This fallacy shifts the focus from evidence and logic to false authority.

For example, "This idea must be true because a celebrity supports it" is an appeal to false authority because it relies on a non-expert rather than evidence.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than false authority. By doing so, we can avoid the appeal to false authority fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Authority

Examples

Business Example: "This strategy must be good because a famous actor endorses it."

Academic Example: "This theory must be true because a popular author supports it."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use false authority to persuade others."

Intimate Partner Example: "You should trust me because I read about it in a magazine."
