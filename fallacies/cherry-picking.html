Cherry Picking

A logical fallacy

Cherry picking occurs when someone selects only evidence that supports their argument while ignoring evidence that contradicts it. This fallacy shifts the focus from a balanced evaluation to a biased selection of data.

For example, "Our product must be great because we've received dozens of positive reviews" is cherry picking if it ignores hundreds of negative reviews.

Critical thinking involves considering all relevant evidence, not just the evidence that supports our preconceptions. By doing so, we can avoid the cherry picking fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Confirmation Bias

Examples

Business Example: "Our sales report looks great because we've only included the top-performing regions."

Academic Example: "This research study is conclusive because we've only considered the data that supports our hypothesis."

Pop-Culture Example: "In documentaries, filmmakers often cherry pick footage to support their narrative."

Intimate Partner Example: "Our relationship is perfect because we never talk about our problems."
