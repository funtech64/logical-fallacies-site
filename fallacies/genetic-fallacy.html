Genetic Fallacy

A logical fallacy

The genetic fallacy occurs when someone judges the truth, goodness, or value of an argument based on its origin or history rather than its current meaning or context. This fallacy shifts the focus from the argument's content to its origin.

For example, "This idea must be wrong because it came from a controversial figure" is a genetic fallacy because it dismisses the idea based on its origin rather than its merits.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on their logical and evidential merits, regardless of their origin. By doing so, we can avoid the genetic fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Ad Hominem

Examples

Business Example: "This business plan must be flawed because it was created by a competitor."

Academic Example: "This research can't be trusted because it was funded by a controversial organization."

Pop-Culture Example: "In movies, characters often dismiss ideas based on their origin rather than their content."

Intimate Partner Example: "This advice must be bad because it came from my ex-partner."
