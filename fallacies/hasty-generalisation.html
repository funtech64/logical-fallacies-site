Hasty Generalisation Fallacy

A logical fallacy

The hasty generalisation fallacy occurs when someone makes a general statement based on a small or unrepresentative sample. This fallacy shifts the focus from sound reasoning to overgeneralised conclusions.

For example, "My friend got sick after eating at that restaurant, so the food must be bad" is an example of a hasty generalisation because it is based on a single instance.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on representative evidence and logical reasoning, rather than overgeneralised conclusions. By doing so, we can avoid the hasty generalisation fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: No True Scotsman

Examples

Business Example: "Our last marketing campaign was unsuccessful, so all future campaigns will be unsuccessful."

Academic Example: "One study found no effect, so the theory must be wrong."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use hasty generalisations to jump to conclusions."

Intimate Partner Example: "You forgot our anniversary, so you don't care about our relationship."
