False Dilemma (Bifurcation Fallacy)

A logical fallacy

The false dilemma, or bifurcation fallacy, occurs when someone presents two options as the only possible choices, ignoring other alternatives. This fallacy shifts the focus from sound reasoning to a limited and often misleading set of choices.

For example, "You're either with us or against us" is an example of a false dilemma because it ignores other possible stances.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on all available options and evidence, rather than a limited set of choices. By doing so, we can avoid the false dilemma fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Sweeping Generalisation

Examples

Business Example: "We must either lower prices or go out of business."

Academic Example: "You must either accept my theory or reject all of science."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often use false dilemmas to create dramatic tension."

Intimate Partner Example: "You either love me or you don't care about me at all."
