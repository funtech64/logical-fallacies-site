Wishful Thinking

A logical fallacy

Wishful thinking occurs when someone believes that something is true or will happen simply because they want it to be true. This fallacy shifts the focus from evidence to desires and hopes.

For example, "I believe I will get the job because I really want it" is wishful thinking because it relies on desire rather than qualifications or evidence.

Critical thinking involves evaluating arguments based on evidence and logical reasoning, rather than personal desires. By doing so, we can avoid the wishful thinking fallacy and maintain a focus on the issues at hand.

See also: Appeal to Emotion

Examples

Business Example: "Our new product will be a success because we really need it to be."

Academic Example: "I will pass this exam because I have to graduate."

Pop-Culture Example: "In TV shows, characters often rely on wishful thinking to solve their problems."

Intimate Partner Example: "Our relationship will work out because I want it to."
